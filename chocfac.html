Chocolate Factory


  This was an individual project and involved exploring the potential re-use of existing buildings to a Multi-Disciplinary Design Studio.

  The client is a community of designers, artists, and small enterprises from different disciplines that wish to work together in a creative and stimulating environment. They include an interior designer, a graphic designer, furniture designer, filmmaker, photographer, event planner, jeweler, fine artist, chocolatier, and web designer.

  They are excited by the idea of sharing ideas and collaborating on projects together. They want the building they work in to impress their clients as soon as they enter the front door. They have seen similar projects in London, Stockholm, Berlin, New York and they would like to carefully examine innovative office design.

  The clients would like the environmental principle of the building to be one of the leading design features. The energy strategy and materials should be thoughtfully selected.

  The reception, cafe, and event space should create revenue to self-fund the community and creative startups.

  The idea of a flexible 2 story space between ground/first floors is something that should be considered. The space should have the capacity to be divided up if required to maximize use.

  The community would like you to consider that some designers might require more space than others and some more privacy than others, so ideally, there should be some variations within the studio space. Provide meeting/breakouts spaces where collaboration can occur/happen easily.

  Your client wishes you to consider branding, logos, and signages as part of the overall design to reflect thethe innovation of the designers and catch the eye of the passing foot traffic.
