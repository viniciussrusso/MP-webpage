Red Stables


  The client, a Development Company that has acquired a rural building, The Red Stables, wants to transform it into a comfortable and welcoming space for all prople. The client wishes to adapt a historic building to a residential apartment house complex for people of all ages. The apartments should be a mix of sizes going from studios to three bedrooms apartment. The client wants the architect to be conscious of the historical value of the building so as to avoid unnecessary damage to the original envelope. All extensions should be easily removable.

  The client wishes that the construction of the new apartments have a low carbon footprint and with the use of smart technologies to turn the structure into a near passive housing. Also to be included renewable sources of energy and waste treatments.

  All the apartments should be thought trough universal design and be future proof — also one apartment specifically designed for a person of special needs or disability. The design is to include a Design Solution for Positive Change. All interior design concept must consists of research and a design solution for positive change. To include community space for the community to use and an exterior interactive space/feature for the greater good of the city.

  The client would like the complex to be a leading example of how this type of Apartment House should work and want research on international examples of best practice. Consider the use of cutting edge smart home technologies as in furniture, fittings, materials and lightning.
